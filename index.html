Morioka: Hidden Gem Chosen by the New York Times

Picking Up Accommodations in the City with a Growing Tourist Population
Mayumi Oshige | July 7, 2023 | Github

When you think of traveling to Japan, the first tourist destination that comes to mind is probably Metropolitan Tokyo, historical and traditional Kyoto or neighboring Osaka. Perhaps, Okinawa which is a resort island also known for the U.S. military bases, Hiroshima or Hokkaido if you try your best to think of it.

In January this year, the New York Times listed “Morioka” as one of the 52 places in the world to visit in 2023 second only to London.

Morioka? Where is that?

No wonder if you think that way. Even Japanese were surprised by the result because Morioka was rather taken as one of the ordinary mid-sized cities that exist all over Japan.

Morioka is the capital of Iwate Prefecture in northeastern Japan. It has a population of about 300,000 and an area of about 900 square kilometers — roughly two-thirds the size of New York City. Winters are cold and snowy.

The New York Times chose Morioka because of its combination of kindness, cuisine, walkability and history. (Read the New York Times article for more details). Indeed, Tokyo and Kyoto are so crowded that they are tiresome and difficult to walk around.

The article made Japanese realize the unknown attractions of Morioka and think of tourism in a different light for cities that may have hidden potential instead of being thought of as unattractive to foreigners. As a matter of fact, Fukuoka, the city located in Kyushu island in western Japan was also chosen for one of the 52 places as well.

According to the local report, the number of foreign tourists to Morioka has been slowly increasing after the New York Times article appeared. A new tourist information center was reportedly set up in front of Morioka Station. The number of foreign tourists visiting Japan had been growing rapidly before the pandemic. In May, Covid-19 was downgraded to a non-threatening status and visits have been on a recovery trend.

Morioka is expected to see an increase in travelers in the future, but how many accommodations are available? Using the API of Rakuten Travel, a major Japanese travel site, we found 31 hotels in Morioka.

They are concentrated in front of Morioka Station, and some appear to be traditional ryokan inns along the Kitakami River where visitors can enjoy hot springs. Prices are reasonable, with the lowest price averaging 4,500 yen, and the hotel review ratings average 4.145 out of 5.

Morioka Mayor Hiroaki Tanifuji said, "I would like to thank the New York Times and reporter Craig Modo once again for shining a light on Morioka's charms and giving it a high rating. We will continue to make every effort to ensure that Morioka remains an attractive city”.
